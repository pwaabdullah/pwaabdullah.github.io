<!-- Short Bio -->
<section class="bio-section short-bio">
  <h2>Short</h2>
  <p>
    <strong>Abdullah Al Mamun, PhD</strong> is a distinguished AI researcher and industry expert in Recommender Systems (RecSys) and Generative AI (GenAI). He is currently a <strong>Sr. ML Engineer at Atlassian</strong> on the <strong>Central AI team</strong>, where he builds and improves <strong>SMART Answer</strong> generation for Jira/Confluence Search using <strong>RL-based fine-tuned LLMs</strong> and <strong>multi-Agent AI architecture</strong>.
  </p>
  <p>
    Previously, he was a <strong>Member of Technical Staff at Aisera</strong>, architecting end-to-end <strong>multi-agentic AI systems</strong> for enterprise IT/HR, driving <strong>~$3.4M estimated ARR</strong>, fine-tuning <strong>LLaMA-3</strong> on AWS to save <strong>$4M in CAPEX</strong> vs. GPT-4, and enhancing the RAG pipeline for a <strong>~93%</strong> real-time semantic-search improvement. Before that, he was a <strong>Machine Learning Engineer at Meta</strong>, fine-tuning <strong>LLaMA 3</strong> for large-scale ad creation (<strong>13% CTR ↑, 6% CVR ↑, ~$497M iRev</strong>) and leading <strong>AutoCA</strong> audience clustering for personalized ads ranking (<strong>~$109M iRev</strong>).
  </p>
  <p>
    Academically, he holds a <strong>PhD in Computer Science from Florida International University</strong>, focused on <strong>interpretable applied ML</strong> for early cancer detection and drug recommendation. His journey spans research, academia, and production-level AI systems, with deep expertise in <strong>LLMs</strong>, <strong>Transformers</strong>, <strong>RAG</strong>, <strong>RL fine-tuning</strong>, and scalable ML systems.
  </p>
</section>

<!-- Long Bio -->
<section class="bio-section long-bio">
  <h2>Long</h2>
  <p>
    <strong>Abdullah Al Mamun, PhD</strong>, has spent over a decade at the forefront of ML research and applied AI, with a mission to bridge academic excellence and industry-scale impact. His journey spans multiple countries, top-tier institutions, and FAANG, culminating in his current role at <strong>Atlassian</strong> shaping the future of enterprise agentic AI for Jira and Confluence Search.
  </p>
  <p>
    He began his academic career in <strong>Bangladesh</strong>, earning a <strong>BS in CSE from Dhaka University of Engineering and Technology (DUET)</strong>, where his early interest in machine learning and natural language processing (NLP) took root. Pursuing deeper expertise, he moved to <strong>KSA</strong>, earning a <strong>Master’s in Computer Engineering from King Fahd University of Petroleum & Minerals (KFUPM)</strong>. During this period, he developed an LSTM-based sentiment analysis system that achieved 98% accuracy on customer feedback data.
  </p>
  <p>
    In <strong>2017</strong>, he joined <strong>Qatar University</strong> as a machine learning researcher and went on to win the <strong>2nd GCC Robotics Challenge</strong>, a milestone that recognized his innovation in AI and robotics.
  </p>
  <p>
    Later that year, Abdullah moved to the <strong>United States</strong> to pursue a <strong>PhD in CS at Florida International University (FIU)</strong>. His research focused on interpretable deep learning for early-stage cancer detection and drug recommendation. His work resulted in multiple publications and travel fellowships to premier conferences such as ACM BCB and IEEE BIBM, reflecting both academic rigor and translational impact.
  </p>
  <p>
    In <strong>2022</strong>, Abdullah transitioned to industry, joining <strong>Meta</strong> (formerly Facebook) as a <strong>Machine Learning Engineer</strong>. At Meta, he worked on large-scale <strong>ads ranking, personalization, and generative ad creation</strong> using cutting-edge techniques like <strong>MTML</strong> and <strong>Transformer-based sequence learning models</strong>. He collaborated with Meta AI to integrate LLMs into ad-creation workflows by fine-tuning <strong>LLaMA 3</strong> (SFT, RLHF, KV-Cache, 4D parallelism, quantization, distillation) and delivering <strong>13% CTR ↑</strong>, <strong>6% CVR ↑</strong>, and <strong>~$497M in incremental revenue</strong>. He also led <strong>AutoCA audience clustering</strong> for ads ranking via targeting relaxation, contributing another <strong>0.1% iRev improvement (~$109M)</strong>.
  </p>
  <p>
    In <strong>2024</strong>, Abdullah joined <strong>Aisera</strong> as a <strong>Member of Technical Staff</strong>, where he led the design and deployment of <strong>multi-agentic AI systems</strong> for enterprise IT and HR automation, driving <strong>~$3.4M estimated ARR</strong>. He architected a scalable, reusable onboarding agent and spearheaded the migration from commercial APIs (e.g., GPT-4) to in-house <strong>fine-tuned LLaMA-3 models</strong> on AWS, saving <strong>$4M in CAPEX</strong>. He also enhanced the <strong>RAG pipeline</strong>, improving real-time semantic search performance by <strong>~93%</strong>.
  </p>
  <p>
    Most recently, Abdullah joined <strong>Atlassian</strong> as a <strong>Sr. ML Engineer</strong> on the <strong>Central AI team</strong>, where he builds and improves <strong>SMART Answer</strong> generation for <strong>Jira and Confluence Search</strong>. His work centers on <strong>RL-based fine-tuned LLMs</strong> and <strong>multi-Agent AI architecture</strong>, driving the next generation of enterprise search experiences for millions of users across thousands of organizations.
  </p>
  <p>
    Throughout his career, Abdullah has developed deep expertise in <strong>RecSys</strong>, <strong>LLMs</strong>, <strong>Transformers</strong>, <strong>RAG</strong>, <strong>vector databases</strong>, <strong>PyTorch</strong>, <strong>Hugging Face</strong>, <strong>LangChain</strong>, and <strong>inference optimization</strong>. He holds certifications from <strong>Google Cloud</strong> and the <strong>University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign</strong>, and continues to contribute actively to the field through open-source, research, and real-world deployment of AI systems.
  </p>
  <p>
    Now based in <strong>Fremont, California</strong>, he remains focused on building AI that is not only intelligent, but scalable, reliable, and transformative for enterprises and society alike.
  </p>
  <p>
    Outside of work, <strong>Abdullah</strong> leads an active lifestyle with a love for <strong>badminton, skiing, hiking, and mountain biking</strong>. He is also passionate about <strong>nature photography</strong> and has a deep appreciation for the outdoors. A frequent traveler, he has visited <strong>16+ countries</strong> so far and continues to explore new cultures and landscapes to fuel his curiosity and creativity.
  </p>
</section>
